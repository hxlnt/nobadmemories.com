No cash value, no bad memories

Anime Land (2/2), 2012

In the 1980s, "No Bad Memories" was the slogan of an American floppy-disk manufacturer. The phrase now serves as a design mantra for electronic artist Rachel Simone Weil, whose work turns a playful yet critical eye toward computer and video game nostalgia. Why are contemporary retrocomputing aesthetics so congruent? What viewpoints does that congruence privelege or marginalize? Weil's work follows a critical design practice to reimagine obsolete technology against the grain of mainstream, commercial nostalgia. By confusing and challenging our visions of the past, these works invite us to dream of better futures.
